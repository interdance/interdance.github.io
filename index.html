Abstract

Humans can perform a variety of interactive motion, among which two-person dance is one of the most challenging interactions. However, in terms of computer motion generation, current work is still unable to generate high-quality interactive actions, especially in the field of double dance. On the one hand, this is caused by the lack of large-scale high-quality data sets, and on the other hand, it is caused by interactive motion generation algorithms. In order to solve the above challenges, we proposed a large-scale two-person dance data set containing rich styles and whole-body movements, totaling 7.85 hours; we also proposed a new motion representation and an interactive dance generation network based on diffusion model. Experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of our dataset and algorithm.
